Repairs & Maintenance

Understanding Your Rights as a Tenant

As a tenant, you have the right to live in a property that is safe, secure, and in good repair. Your landlord is responsible for maintaining the structure and exterior of your home, as well as keeping the installations for water, gas, electricity, sanitation, and heating in proper working order.

Steps to Request Repairs

  1. Identify the issue: Take photos and notes about the problem.
  2. Notify your landlord: Contact them in writing, describing the issue clearly.
  3. Allow reasonable time: Your landlord should respond within a reasonable timeframe.
  4. Follow up: If no action is taken, send a formal repair notice.
  5. Seek assistance: If repairs are still not done, contact your local housing authority.

Sample Repair Request Letter

[Your Name]
[Your Address]
[City, State ZIP]
[Date]

[Landlord's Name]
[Landlord's Address]
[City, State ZIP]

Dear [Landlord's Name],

I am writing to formally request repairs for [describe the issue] at my rental property located at [your address]. The issue began on [date] and affects my ability to [explain impact].

According to our rental agreement and housing regulations, this repair falls under your responsibility as the property owner. I would appreciate if this matter could be addressed within [timeframe, e.g., 14 days].

Please contact me at [your phone number] or [your email] to arrange a convenient time for the repairs to be carried out.

Thank you for your prompt attention to this matter.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]

Faulty Products & Warranty

Understanding Your Consumer Rights

As a consumer, you have the right to receive products that are of satisfactory quality, fit for purpose, and as described. When a product develops a fault or doesn't work as expected, you are protected by consumer laws that give you the right to a repair, replacement, or refund.

Steps to Take When You Have a Faulty Product

  1. Act quickly: Report the issue to the seller as soon as you notice the problem. Many warranties and return policies have time limits.
  2. Gather evidence: Take photos of the fault and collect any relevant documents (receipt, warranty, product manual).
  3. Contact the seller: Speak with the retailer first, not the manufacturer, as your contract is with the seller.
  4. Put it in writing: If the initial contact doesn't resolve the issue, send a formal complaint letter.
  5. Escalate if necessary: If your complaint isn't resolved, consider contacting a consumer protection agency or seeking legal advice.

Understanding Warranties

There are two types of warranties you should know about:

  • Express warranty: The written warranty provided by the manufacturer or seller.
  • Implied warranty: Legal protections that exist regardless of whether a written warranty is provided.

Even if the express warranty has expired, you may still be protected by implied warranty laws for major defects.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]

My Question

Posted on: May 15, 2023 Answered

My landlord hasn't fixed my heating for 3 weeks. I've sent multiple emails and called twice. It's getting very cold in my apartment. What are my rights in this situation?

Volunteer Response:

Answered on: May 16, 2023 By: Housing Advisor Sarah

Thank you for your question. I'm sorry to hear about your heating issue. This is a serious matter as heating is considered an essential service.

Here are the steps you should take:

  1. Send a formal repair notice: Although you've contacted your landlord verbally and by email, I recommend sending a formal written repair notice via certified mail. This creates an official record.
  2. Specify a reasonable timeframe: In your notice, give the landlord 7 days to fix the heating (this is generally considered reasonable for essential services).
  3. Contact your local housing authority: If the landlord fails to respond to your formal notice, you can file a complaint with your local housing or health department.
  4. Consider repair and deduct: In some areas, tenants can hire someone to fix essential services and deduct the cost from rent, but check your local laws before doing this.

I've attached a template for a formal repair notice that you can use. Remember to keep copies of all communications and take photos of the thermostat if possible to document the issue.

Feel free to follow up if you need additional guidance after sending the formal notice.

Phone & SMS Scams

This page will help you understand how to identify common phone and SMS scams and protect yourself from fraud.

Common Types of Scams / Scenarios

Phone Scams:

  1. Calls impersonating the ATO, Medicare, or Centrelink
  2. Fake bank representatives asking to “verify your account” or “freeze your funds”
  3. Voicemail messages claiming you've won a prize or missed a delivery

SMS Scams:

  1. Fake tracking links from “Australia Post” or courier companies
  2. Messages demanding fine or tax payments with suspicious links
  3. Phishing links requesting bank or identity information

How to Spot a Scam

  1. Authorities will never ask for passwords or codes over the phone or by SMS
  2. Legitimate institutions won't threaten with “immediate arrest” or “account freezes”
  3. Do not click unknown links or call unfamiliar numbers
  4. Look out for strange wording, typos, or suspicious sender numbers

What to Do If You Receive One

  1. Hang up immediately; do not reply or click anything
  2. Visit official websites to verify information
  3. Report the scam to Scamwatch or relevant authorities
  4. Block the number and consider using anti-scam apps

Online & Shopping Scams

How to identify fake shopping websites and phishing emails.

Common Scenarios

  1. Fake online stores that never deliver products
  2. Emails pretending to be from eBay, Amazon, or PayPal asking for login details
  3. Ads on social media for too-good-to-be-true deals
  4. Scams involving cryptocurrency products or fake electronics

How to Spot Them

  1. No contact information or unclear return policies
  2. Poor grammar and blurry logos on websites
  3. Requests for payment via gift cards or wire transfer
  4. URLs that look similar to real sites but slightly off (e.g. amaz0n.com)

What to Do If You're a Victim

  1. Cancel payment and report to your bank
  2. Report the website/email to Scamwatch
  3. Change passwords if you entered your login information
  4. Share the experience to help others avoid the same trap

Dating & Investment Scams

Beware of 'friends' asking for money online or 'investment opportunities' promising high returns.

Common Scenarios

  1. Romance scams on dating websites or social media
  2. Fake military personnel claiming to be overseas
  3. Ponzi schemes or “crypto investment opportunities”
  4. People who suddenly ask for urgent money transfers

Red Flags

  1. Someone you've never met asking for money
  2. Emotional stories designed to gain sympathy
  3. Promises of guaranteed high returns with no risk
  4. Pressure to act quickly or keep things secret

What to Do

  1. Never send money to someone you haven't met
  2. Reverse image search their photos
  3. Talk to friends or family before making decisions
  4. Report the scam to Scamwatch and the platform

Unexpected Bills & Prize Scams

What to do when you receive bills for services you never subscribed to or messages congratulating you on winning

Common Scenarios

  1. Fake bills for antivirus software, tech support, or unpaid tolls
  2. SMS claiming you've won a prize or lottery
  3. Robocalls about winning a cruise or tax refund
  4. Subscription charges for services never signed up for

How to Spot Them

  1. You never requested the service
  2. Vague company names with no website
  3. Prizes that require “small upfront fees”
  4. Threats like “final notice” or “legal action”

What to Do If You Receive One

  1. Don't click any links or call back unknown numbers
  2. Check bank statements and dispute unauthorized charges
  3. Report scams to your provider or Scamwatch
  4. Block the number or sender
